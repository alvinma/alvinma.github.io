Japan - September 26th to October 10th, 2019

The amazing Japan highlights!

It has been a while since I have gone on an oversea trip, and this trip was a huge success in my endevors as it had opened up my mind and made me want to travel to world a whole lot more. The culture, food, cleanliness, infrastructure; everything was astouding!! My experience in Japan makes me want to see what the rest of the world has to offer. Let me not bore you with any more details. Let me show you what I was able to capture during my experience in Japan...

Insert a collage of awesome experiences of Japan here.

On My Way to Japan!!

It has been a while since I had flown internationally, but this experience brought a wonderful experience! The greatest lesson I picked up was to travel with no, or close to none, expectations!! My flight was with United Airlines and my expectations on this flight was extremely low as I only heard about not so great things about flying with United Airlines, but even though I had the middle seat of a three seat row, it wasn't too bad. The mother and baby on my left was pretty nice and interesting to watch, the guy, Matthew, to my right was travelling to Tokyo only for the four day weekend for his girlfriend (True commitment right there!!), and I had a lot of movies and anime to watch!

The food wasn't bad either! One of my concerns was that the food would be unbarable, but because of my low expectations, the food ended up being pretty edible. I was also served a lemon serbert for desert! It was too sweet and I was not able to finish it, but the effort was seriously appreciated. Overall, it was a descent flight. And on the plus side, Alladin live action movie was pretty entertaining to watch!

Insert plane photo here.

As soon as I landed in Tokyo, my adventure begins and I spent the rest of the day figuring out the infrastructure of Japan. The subway system was just simply impressive, but once you consistently take the train, you'll be able to master it in two days as there is literally only one line that you'll take over and over again. The Yamamoto Line for the WIN!

To relinquish a crave I had since I last gone to Japan in 2007, I directly went to Ichiran Ramen, lined up for thirty minutes, and ordered the largest order they had! Yes! My first meal was Ichiran Ramen and it was delicious!!

Sad Realization of Tokyo...

September 27th, 2019 - October 3rd, 2019

On my first day at Toyko, I was excited to go out and roam the city in expectation that the city was similar in size to a nearby home city, San Francisco... After my first day of exploration, I was surprised to find out that Tokyo was nothing like San Francisco. Toyko was ENORMOUS compared to San Francisco! After some research, San Francisco is roughly 47 square miles (121.73 square kilometers), whereas Tokyo is roughly 845 square miles (2188.55 square kilometers)!! This was just estonishing! I was not able cover a piece of Tokyo in the day, and there was just so many activities happening everyday!

Even after altering the Japan trip and removing Kitakata, the ramen city, from the itinery to spend more time in Japan, seven days was still not enough time to explore the entire city of Tokyo to the fullest! In the days I spent in Tokyo, I was only able to cover Shibuya, Shinjuku, Koto, Chiyoda, Chuo, Mitaka, and Musashino wards of Tokyo [Note: Wards are subdivisions of a city]. In a week's time, I was only able to hit up seven wards of Tokyo, and I still wasn't able to fully explore each one of them. There is just too many things that can be done in Japan! I just kept going back and forth between each ward each day just to keep up with what I booked and the unexpected festivities going on!

In my adventures, I may have accidentally stumble upon Kawasaki City and ended up in the Kawasaki Ward for half a day. The area had more of a gambling feel to it. It may have been because I entered into multiple casinos out of curiosity of what kind of games they offer in Japan.

Shibuya

Shibuya was pretty amazing! It was pretty much a huge shopping district! There were so many things to see, buy, and eat!! This was the first spot I hit up a sushi restaurant and it was AMAZING! The dollar per plate deal for fresh fish was AMAZING! I would come back to Japan just for the food!

With Shabuya, there is an All You Can Eat hotpot called Shabuya back at home, meaning that I just had to try the All You Can Eat hotpot here in Shabuya, Japan! They offer beef tongue as a dish in Japan and it was delicious!!

I am not a big shopper, but there were so many stores around the area. Especially second-hand thrift shops. They had different style of clothing for days! Secondhand brand named clothes were sitting being glass cabinets for admiration purposes. It was very different from what I am accustomed to.

Other places I found very interesting about Shabuya was that there was always an event going on. Everytime I visited Yoyogi Park, some kind of festival is going on. Out of the three times I have been to Shabuya and explored near Yoyogi Park, I was able to join in a world cultures, Indian, and street food market festivals! Shabuya was an area where the excitement does not seem to fade!

Shinjuku

Shinjuku consist of a beautiful Gyoen National Botanic Garden! The was just a vast area of green grass, trees, and a few greenhouses. People were setting up picnics or simply taking a nap in the fresh outdoors. It was an amazing place to be!

Of course, there was the other side of Shinjuku. The red light district was pretty interesting and many activities occurs in this distric of Shinjuku. The Robot Restaurant which I had tickets for was in this district and as a tourist, it was one of the most exciting shows to watch! I loved it! But in the district itself, you'll have Japanese men in business suits or Nigerian men coming up to you, trying to get you to go to a specific bar. Honestly, I wanted to see what it felt like to be approach and hassled to go to a certain bar, but it was not as extreme as I had enticipated. This was probably due to the fact that the Rugby World Cup was going on in Japan and literally ALL the bars at this district was filled up by the fans.

Koto

Koto ward was pretty fun! The monorail ran throughout the area and it felt like an open amusement park. Every station had something different going on and things to see. TeamLab Borderless, the fancy destination with all the lights, could be found here. A large ferris wheel can be taken here to have a view of the area. Odaiba Statue of Liberty Republic can be found here. The large two stories Gundam Unicorn can be found here; and if you waited long enough, you can witness the statue change modes!! There are many many gardens around the area. And finally, the Toyota showcase is here as well. This is where they display all their cars, 4D experinece rides, and future product demonstrations!

Chiyoda

I happened to stubble into Chiyoda City because I noticed a HUGE park in the middle of the city. After my first meal in Japan at Ichiran Ramen and munching on Mcdonald's fries, this park was refreshing to stroll through at night. It was a lot bigger than I expected and many of the park were closed off at night due to security reasons. Even so, I saw a lot of joggers and runners taking advantage of the openess of this park and taking a nightly job/run throughout the park. The nature plus patches of water made the area a nice place to visit. The Imperial Palace, Shin-Marunouchi Building, and Seimon Stonebridge were nice structures to be seen in this area.

Another big attraction at Chiyoda Ward is the famous area for anime, Akihabara!! Anime could be seen everywhere!! There were specific kinds of anime and gaming cafes, stores with many figure displays, and people in cosplay walking around. The Japanese culture is very open, so if you think about what kind of anime figures, pillow, or cards you may want to find in these stores, you may actually find it.

Akihabara is also known as the Electric City of Tokyo Japan as they have many electronics shops, game cafes, and arcades! Where ever you go, you will find a store with many electronic goods or a place with so many claw machines, you would not know how big the building actually is.. This district is a very interesting palce to visit.

Chuo

I bypassed this area multiple times! Chuo ward is where the fish market and Tsukiji street market is held. There is a lot of fresh foods here, but I have to say, even though the street foods were priced higher than I expected, they tasted AMAZING!! Specifically, I was really shocked by how good the large shelled oysters tasted. It was the best oyster I've ever had and I couldn't find anything better elsewhere! Sadly, the fish market wasn't something I was expected to see, but if you are willing to wake up early enough, you may be able to witness the auction going on. This is something I tried a few times, but couldn't do...

Mitaka/Musashino

Because I wanted to try an animal cafe, I ended up going to Inokashira Park in Mitaka Ward. It was the first park I visited in Japan during the day and it surprised me of how many families were having quality time together out at this park. With a big lake, there were swan paddle boat rides and families, couples, and friends were riding them throughout the lake as a casual stroll. It was really eye opening of the quality of life people in Japan has. Families are extremely active in Japan as well as I saw a lot of people doing physical activities with each other. Mothers were swining a jump rope for her kids, and fathers were doing push-ups with their son. The Ghibli Museum could be found here as well! When I accidentally found myself inside the museum without permission during closing (because I didn't know what I could access), I found it pretty funny how they had a giant Totoro at the entrance of the museum acting as a receptionist. The museum also had a real natural feeling to it as if it was abandoned and plants started growing along the walls of the museum. This museum is like a large playhouse, but no touching!

After strolling through the park, I ended up going to Cat Cafe Temari no Ouchi in Musashino. As this was my second day in Japan, I was quite confused as to where I was going. You would find stores everywhere, but the confusing part was that stores would be on different levels. Using google maps helped me pinpoint the location of the Cat Cafe, but I ended up going back and forth until I realized that the cafe was on the third floor. This gave me quite the perspective of the country's infrastructure and assisted me locating many other spots in the city. At the end, I was able to meet some cute cats wearing costumes, enjoyed some window shopping, and learned a valuable lesson to always look up when finding a specific destination!

Kawasaki

When I stubbled into Kawasaki Ward accidentally, I was not expecting too much as the city was initially dead during the day. There wasn't as many people as I enticipated to be roaming around the city, but the riverbed between Tokyo and Kawasaki was pleasant to walk along. As I was about to leave the city near sundown, I started to notice a lot of people. Casinos were more visible and booming, shopping centers were on full display, and I accidentally ran into a mini waterfountain musical show! A mini Bellagio water show! The Kawasaki Ward simply reminded me of a smaller version of Las Vegas!

My Life as a Bum!!

October 3rd, 2019 - October 4th, 2019

That's right!! In Japan, I experienced a life as a bum! A homeless person that slept at the bus stop/train station for the night! It was truly an an experience!

Mt. Fuji + Lake Kawaguchiko

October 4th, 2019 - October 6th, 2019

Due to the homeless experience I had, I was not able to fully utilize my time at Mt. Fuji or Lake Kawaguchiko. Therefore, for the full day I had at Fujiyoshida area, I decided to rent a bike for the day and ride the 30 mile loop around Lake Kawaguchiko. This way, I would be able to get a picture of the lake with Mt. Fuji in it while being able to try out the local cuisines!

The Bullet to Hokaido!!

October 6th, 2019

I had higher expecations for the bullet train, but it was a lot slower than I enticipated. Going at 200km/hr may be fast, but I was expecting it to go a tad bit faster over the years. Overall, the ride was smooth, I was able to sleep, and I was able to enjoy my bento boxes, which were amazing and an experience to have! Highly recommend the bento boxes in Japan for long traveling trips and picnics!!

Hokkaido

October 6th, 2019 - October 10th, 2019

Hakkkaido is one of the most natural places I have been to. Majority of the cities here are like small towns with small culture engulfed with lush green life. It is like a natural resort in every stay in Hokkaido.

Hokkaido was prety much a planned relaxation day after a lot of exploration and binge eating that was done in Tokyo. With Hokkaido known to have the best spots for onsens (Hot Springs), these cities were the perfect last spots to hit! The plan was that we hit up the onsens in Hakodate, Toya, and Noroibetsu for the best onsen experiences while eating the best seafood in Japan, and end the trip with a shopping spree at Sapporo for gifts and the many goodies we could stuff in a bag to bring home. After walking between 18 - 26 miles (28.96 - 41.84 km) a day on the days prior, these was a grand treat to dip my toe in some hot water and relax.

Granted that this was my first onsen experience and I was pretty shy and worrisome to the Japanese onsen customs, the experience was really pleasant! In all three different onsen experiences, I admit. I had my concerns with the traditional customs, but after dipping in the hot water, all worries goes away and nothing mattered anymore after a few minutes.

Again, this was my first time and I didn't know what to expect in each experience, but my worries did not supsied because the onsen was very soothing and relaxing. It was soothing and relaxing, but it was too freaking HOT!!!! I was literally losing my mind sitting in the onsen, but the rest of my body was in such a relax state that I didn't want to move. It was such a weird and interesting experience.

Now to talk about the food, I actually had the best experience in food experience in Hokkaido! Food was just amazing and I was able to sample on many of Hokkaido's delights!

Hakodate

October 6th, 2019 - October 7th, 2019

Hakodate is a city known to have the best seafood in Japan, but what I discovered here was that the famous Shiyo Ramen in Hakodate was the best ramen I have ever tasted! The broth was super light, the wontons were small and flavorful, and the chasu was glissering with a good layer of fat. I would have to say that this light broth ramen beats wonton noodle soup and pho easily! It was extremely flavorful! The best quote/situation that I will never forget my friend had gone through; "I'm still so full from the bento boxes.. Let me finish only the wontons and chasu.." A few minutes later.. "WHAT HAPPENED TO ALL THE NOODLES!?!!?? THEY JUST DISAPPEARED!!! I HAD A LOT OF NOODLES LEFT!!!". This situation and more can fully describe how much I enjoyed this simple bowl of shiyo ramen!

Toya

October 7th, 2019 - October 8th, 2019

Lake Toya was gorgeous, but what really made this town great, and also my Japan trip, was the awesome experience I had at Lake Toya Resort! From the customer service once we arrived to the hotel, to the cleanliness, to the decorations, to the western/eastern combo room, to the all you can eat dinner and breakfast buffer, to the amazing outdoors lake view hot spring. Everything was simply amazing and made one simple day a dream come true! From my experiences with the ~$200 hotels per person back at home, the experience is pretty much mediocure, but in Japan, every penny spent was worth it!

Noboribetsu

October 8th, 2019 - October 9th, 2019

Noboribetsu was a simple city, but this was the only time during the trip where the weather had limited what we were able to do. With the heavy rainfall, we simply stayed in our rooms, relaxed, and enjoyed the hot springs whenever we could. This city seemed a little too old fassioned for my taste, and I realized this after entering the onsen (hot spring) and finding A LOT of old men enjoying the onsen. I would be okay to work with a few, but this was an uncomfortable amount of old men huddled up in one small outdoors onsen. I would have to admit, the outdoor onsen had a beautiful view with a waterfall and a lot of Bonsai trees around, but with roughly twenty men huddled around in a onsen fully naked, this was just a bit too much. The indoor onsen was enormous and enjoyable, but it wasn't what I wanted to experience. Overall, this stay wasn't too bad and the all you can eat buffet was not as good as Lake Toya Resort, but it was stil better than any other buffets I have ever had!

Sapporo

October 9th, 2019 - October 10th, 2019

With my trip coming to the end, Sapporo was designated as the day that I shop for everything I found and wanted! This was the time I started shopping for clothes, candy, snacks, unique electronics, and souvenirs. Of course, I had to tour the city first and see what the city had to offer! Moerenuma Park was the first stop I went to and it was pretty much the biggest interactive park I have been to. There were so many parks within this park! During the time I had gone, I climbed up Mt. Moere and the wind was strong enough to push me around if I did not shift my body weight around or sit down! I was quite amazed by the feeling as I am pretty heavy.

Afterwards, it was time to explore for some food options, but firstly, I had to try some beer from the Sapporo Beer Museum. Here, I discovered that Sapporo Black Star beer brand was pretty tasty compared to the others. After drinking a few glasses of beer, it was time to fill up my stomach with food, and since it was my last day, I had to try everything I could think of! I was ready to binge eat again! Crepe was the first item in my stomach. Then the well known Sappro dish, Kangaskan lamb. And finally, more ramen!! It was a wonderful day where I shopped until 2am in the morning at the multiple Donqui Megastore.

Did I forget to mention, I visited the Tanukikoji Shopping Arcade? This was basically a long, pedestrian only, shopping street that consists of 200 shops! The best part was, the hostel that was booked was conveniently in the center of this arcade! It was the best "shop and quickly unload" experience I had! My wallet was hit many times on this day..
