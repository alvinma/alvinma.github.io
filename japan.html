Japan - September 26th to October 10th, 2019

The amazing Japan highlights!

It has been a while since I have gone on an oversea trip, and this trip was a huge success in my endevors as it had opened up my mind and made me want to travel to world a whole lot more. The culture, food, cleanliness, infrastructure; everything was astouding!! My experience in Japan makes me want to see what the rest of the world has to offer. Let me not bore you with any more details. Let me show you what I was able to capture during my experience in Japan...

On My Way to Japan!!

It has been a while since I had flown internationally, but this experience brought a wonderful experience! The greatest lesson I picked up was to travel with no, or close to none, expectations!! My flight was with United Airlines and my expectations on this flight was extremely low as I only heard about not so great things about flying with United Airlines, but even though I had the middle seat of a three seat row, it wasn't too bad. The mother and baby on my left was pretty nice and interesting to watch, the guy, Matthew, to my right was travelling to Tokyo only for the four day weekend for his girlfriend (True commitment right there!!), and I had a lot of movies and anime to watch!

The food wasn't bad either! One of my concerns was that the food would be unbarable, but because of my low expectations, the food ended up being pretty edible. I was also served a lemon serbert for desert! It was too sweet and I was not able to finish it, but the effort was seriously appreciated. Overall, it was a descent flight. And on the plus side, Alladin live action movie was pretty entertaining to watch!

Insert plane photo here.

As soon as I landed in Tokyo, my adventure begins and I spent the rest of the day figuring out the infrastructure of Japan. The subway system was just simply impressive, but once you consistently take the train, you'll be able to master it in two days as there is literally only one line that you'll take over and over again. The Yamamoto Line for the WIN!

To relinquish a crave I had since I last gone to Japan in 2007, I directly went to Ichiran Ramen, lined up for thirty minutes, and ordered the largest order they had! Yes! My first meal was Ichiran Ramen and it was delicious!!

Sad Realization of Tokyo...

September 27th, 2019 - October 3rd, 2019

On my first day at Toyko, I was excited to go out and roam the city in expectation that the city was similar in size to a nearby home city, San Francisco... After my first day of exploration, I was surprised to find out that Tokyo was nothing like San Francisco. Toyko was ENORMOUS compared to San Francisco! After some research, San Francisco is roughly 47 square miles (121.73 square kilometers), whereas Tokyo is roughly 845 square miles (2188.55 square kilometers)!! This was just estonishing! I was not able cover a piece of Tokyo in the day, and there was just so many activities happening everyday!

Even after altering the Japan trip and removing Kitakata, the ramen city, from the itinery to spend more time in Japan, seven days was still not enough time to explore the entire city of Tokyo to the fullest! In the days I spent in Tokyo, I was only able to cover Shibuya, Shinjuku, Koto, Chiyoda, Chuo, Ota, and Taito wards of Tokyo [Note: Wards are subdivisions of a city]. In a week's time, I was only able to hit up seven wards of Tokyo, and I still wasn't able to fully explore each one of them. There is just too many things that can be done in Japan! I just kept going back and forth between each ward each day just to keep up with what I booked and the unexpected festivities going on!

In my adventures, I may have accidentally stumble upon Kawasaki City and ended up in the Kawasaki Ward for half a day. The area had more of a gambling feel to it. It may have been because I entered into multiple casinos out of curiosity of what kind of games they offer in Japan.

When I stubbled into Kawasaki Ward accidentally, I was not expecting too much as the city was initially dead during the day. There wasn't as many people as I enticipated to be roaming around the city, but the riverbed between Tokyo and Kawasaki was pleasant to walk along. As I was about to leave the city near sundown, I started to notice a lot of people. Casinos were more visible and booming, shopping centers were on full display, and I accidentally ran into a mini waterfountain musical show! A mini Bellagio water show! The Kawasaki Ward simply reminded me of a smaller version of Las Vegas!

Hokkaido

October 6th, 2019 - October 10th, 2019

Hokkaido was prety much a planned relaxation day after a lot of exploration and binge eating that was done in Tokyo. With Hokkaido known to have the best spots for onsens (Hot Springs), these cities were the perfect last spots to hit! The plan was that we hit up the onsens in Hakodate, Toya, and Noroibetsu for the best onsen experiences while eating the best seafood in Japan, and end the trip with a shopping spree at Sapporo for gifts and the many goodies we could stuff in a bag to bring home. After walking between 18 - 26 miles (28.96 - 41.84 km) a day on the days prior, these was a grand treat to dip my toe in some hot water and relax.

Granted that this was my first onsen experience and I was pretty shy and worrisome to the Japanese onsen customs, the experience was really pleasant! In all three different onsen experiences, I admit. I had my concerns with the traditional customs, but after dipping in the hot water, all worries goes away and nothing mattered anymore after a few minutes.

Again, this was my first time and I didn't know what to expect in each experience, but my worries did not supsied because the onsen was very soothing and relaxing. It was soothing and relaxing, but it was too freaking HOT!!!! I was literally losing my mind sitting in the onsen, but the rest of my body was in such a relax state that I didn't want to move. It was such a weird and interesting experience.

Now to talk about the food, I actually had the best experience in food experience in Hokkaido! Food was just amazing and I was able to sample on many of Hokkaido's delights!
